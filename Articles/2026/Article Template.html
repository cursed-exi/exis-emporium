The Drowned Chapel of Vireholt

Posted on February 21, 2026 | Adventure Location

Last session the party discovered the ruins of a chapel swallowed by marshwater. Locals claim the bells still ring beneath the surface on moonless nights. No one in the village will approach the place after dusk.

[ Image: Exterior of the Drowned Chapel ]

First Impressions

The structure leans slightly eastward. Rot has consumed the wooden beams, yet the stone foundation remains stubbornly intact. The doors hang open. There are no tracks in the mud.

"The water does not reflect the sky here. It reflects the ceiling." — Field notes of Ardin, party wizard

Interior Exploration

The nave is partially flooded. Broken pews float in stagnant water. The altar is intact, though something has scraped markings into its surface. The air smells faintly metallic.

[ Image: Interior, flooded nave and broken pews ]

Notable Features

  • A cracked baptismal font filled with black water.
  • Three stone effigies beneath the chapel floor.
  • A rusted iron bell-rope descending into darkness.
Chapel Guardian (Optional Encounter)
AC: Moderate
HP: Low-Mid
Attack: Drowned grasp (cold damage)
Special: Extinguishes non-magical light within 10 ft
Morale: Fights until submerged

Running the Atmosphere

Keep descriptions quiet and restrained. Emphasize stillness. Avoid immediate combat. Let the players linger. The tension should build from uncertainty rather than danger.

Referee Notes:
  • If players ring the bell, something answers from below.
  • Holy water reacts violently when poured into the font.
  • The effigy's warmth increases when lies are spoken nearby.

The chapel works best as a low-level mystery site. It can also serve as the first clue toward a drowned cult operating beneath the marshlands.

This location was designed to test pacing. The goal is not combat, but hesitation. If your players grow quiet at the table, the chapel is working as intended.
